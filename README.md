# MILP-MMAI
Repo for Cross-modal Alignment and Symbolic Verification of MILPs (Class Project for MMAI 6.S985)

Natural-language interfaces are increasingly used to specify and modify mixed-integer linear programs (MILPs) in interactive optimization systems. However, existing approaches primarily evaluate success through retrieval accuracy or solver outcomes, and/or focus on unimodal (typically tokenized) representation of MILP instances. These approaches may fail to detect subtle but critical mis-specifications between a user’s intent and the resulting formulation. In this project, we study \emph{cross-modal alignment with symbolic consistency checking} for MILP instances: given a natural-language description and a mathematical MILP representation, the goal is to determine whether they are semantically consistent and to localize the source of any mismatch. We frame this as a verification problem rather than a generation task, leveraging structural properties of MILPs that can be extracted deterministically without solving. Our approach compares text-to-text and text-to-graph verification pipelines, and introduces symbolic attribute supervision to enable interpretable and fine-grained diagnosis of modeling errors. Through controlled perturbations and multimodal evaluation, we aim to characterize when structured representations provide stronger signals for detecting logical inconsistencies than purely textual formulations, motivating verification as a critical component of reliable interactive optimization.
